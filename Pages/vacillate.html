Vacillate

Project Overview

One of the annoying problems couples or friends face is trying to decide on what to eat or watch. Instead of going back and forth picking something, what if there was an app that groups can utilize to pick what they like using the same mechanics that dating apps use? Vacillate helps solve this problem. Our goal for this design was to create a simple and easy to use app that utilizes the like or dislike system that dating apps use. Users should be able to cycle through items until a match is received.

The Problem

Groups have difficulties deciding on a place to eat or something to watch, design an app that utilizes dating apps matching system in order to speed up the decision making process and make it easier.

Challenges

The biggest challenge for this project will be making sure the design stays simple and doesn't require too many actions to set up. The app is meant to be quick and easy to use, so an overly complicated workflow can frustrate the user.

Research

The Users

Reasearch was done through online surveys. Particiapants were asked questions pertaining to their favorite hobbies, activities they enjoy doing with others and difficulties faced when planning events. This information was used to create a persona and journey map that will be referred to when designing the application.

Other Products

Most of the inspiration for the design was modeled after dating apps. The designs of three popular dating apps were analyzed to help plan the design. Coffee Meets Bagel proved to be the most useful in planning the structure of the item profiles.

Planning the Design

Informational Architecture and Wireframes

The architecture for the design would be very simple. The application is inteded to do one simple thing, present a list of items that users can like or dislike, but it's important that we include any features the users might need to start matching. For now, there were only be three categories: food, films and TV series. When the user picks an item they like or dislike, they can choose as much as they want until they receive match or they can stop and come back later to continue picking.

Final Design

Invision Studios was used to create the high fidelity prototypes.

Create Groups and Pick a Catergory

Name your group, select dates and start inviting people to your group.

Adjust Group Preferences

Does anyone in the group have specific preferences? Adjust the settings to fit the needs of the group.

Start Matching

See something you like? Give it a heart. Keep going until you find a match or like a few and come back later to see if you matched.

Confirm Your Match

After receiving a match, view matched dates and confirm the match to make sure everyone is good to go.

Style Guide

The design was modeled in the same minimalistic style as dating apps.

Final Takeaway

Orginally, the app was going to include more features, such as the ability to pick near by activities and other complex animations. After receiving feedback on the design, it was said that some of these features would be too difficult to implement due to limitations that will be faced in the back-end. One of the most difficult parts of designing a product is understanding what issues developers might face when implementing the design. While it may be easy to create amazing in prototyping software, it might not be as easy to do for developers. Compromises will have to be made between designers and developers in order to create a well designed product.
